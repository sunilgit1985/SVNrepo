Citi Trends (CTRN) to Repurchase $25 million in Outstanding Stock

May 1st, 2017 - By Amy Steele - 0 comments

Citi Trends (NASDAQ:CTRN) declared that its Board of Directors has approved a share buyback program, which permits the company to buyback $25 million in shares on Monday, April 10th, EventVestor reports. This buyback authorization permits the company to buy shares of its stock through open market purchases. Stock buyback programs are generally an indication that the company’s board believes its shares are undervalued.

Citi Trends (NASDAQ:CTRN) opened at 18.79 on Monday. The firm’s 50-day moving average is $17.81 and its 200-day moving average is $18.32. Citi Trends has a 12 month low of $14.01 and a 12 month high of $22.73. The stock has a market cap of $275.86 million, a P/E ratio of 20.65 and a beta of 0.18.

About Citi Trends

Citi Trends, Inc is a retailer of urban fashion apparel and accessories. The Company’s segment is retail operations. The Company operates approximately 520 stores in both urban and rural markets in over 30 states. The Company offers products under its brands, such as Citi Steps and Red Ape. The Company’s merchandise includes apparel, accessories and home.
